Internship Experience

My internship this past summer working for Explorest as a Business Analyst Intern has also provided me with a wide array of experiences and skills that will be incredibly useful for my future career path. Not only has working for a small start-up given me a glimpse of the inner workings of a fully functioning business, but I have also been able to head projects and complete tasks that have directly impacted the company’s progress and trajectory.

Various projects that I have worked on include: designing and implementing the blog solution for Explorest in order to attract traffic and revenue to the company through content and affiliate marketing, researching VC’s to find potential investment partners, creating scaling and marketing plans for the platform’s imminent launch in California, and devising a content plan that will keep our blog on track and up-to-date in the coming months. Furthermore, I am currently learning how to analyze user data collected from traffic on the mobile application to make future adjustments to the on-boarding flow and subscription model. Working alongside the CEO and other founders has also been a fantastic experience as they have imparted their knowledge and past experiences in the business world.

My Education Background

Going through school in San Jose, CA, I decided to look out of state for my college experience. I chose the University of Michigan as it fit all of my criteria for a college experience. The School of Information at Michigan has allowed me to strengthen both my design and analytical abilities through its diverse course offerings. I have been able to learn invaluable coding languages ranging from Python that will help me parse through large data sets as well as web design languages like HTML and CSS. The UX path has also given me the tools needed to create functioning prototypes that I can improve on with user interviews and testing.

Growing up I have always had a fascination for computers and technology. I enjoy reading up on computer hardware and learning how online applications work through web design and database technology. My interest for the School of Information was helped by this early love for technology and I was curious to learn more behind the science of collecting and interpreting data to make the world a better place.
